Overview

The Brief

Crestline Estates is a personal project designed for a fictional real estate company. The goal was to practice and explore minimalist UI design — creating a site that feels clean and professional without relying on heavy visuals or complex layouts.

The challenge was to strip design down to its essentials while still effectively showcasing property listings, key statistics, and agent profiles.

Process

How I Approached It

01

Wireframing First

Before opening Figma, I sketched the layout on paper — planning the hierarchy, section order, and general structure. Starting low-fidelity helped me focus on layout decisions without getting distracted by aesthetics.

02

Defining the Minimal Direction

I set constraints early: limited color use, generous whitespace, and simple typography. This kept the design focused and prevented scope creep into unnecessary decoration.

03

Building in Figma

With a clear wireframe and direction, I built the full design in Figma — including the hero, property showcase grid, agent profiles, how it works section, and footer.

Key Decisions

Design Choices

Wireframe Before Design

Starting with sketches meant layout decisions were made before visual ones. This kept the structure logical and user-friendly from the beginning.

Restrained Color Use

A limited palette of greens and neutrals keeps the site feeling trustworthy and grounded — important qualities for a real estate brand.

Clear Information Hierarchy

Key stats (properties sold, satisfaction rate) are front and center. The layout guides users naturally toward the most important actions: viewing properties and booking visits.

Minimal but Complete

The design is simple, but nothing important is missing. Property cards, agent profiles, a process section, and testimonials are all present — just presented without unnecessary decoration.

Outcome

Personal project. A self-directed design exercise focused on practicing minimalist UI principles applied to a real estate context. Designed fully in Figma.
