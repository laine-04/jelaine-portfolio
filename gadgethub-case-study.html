Overview

The Brief

GadgetHub is a rental device app I designed during my internship. We were given a flowchart that laid out the full rental process, from login and KYC verification, to browsing devices, checking availability, payment, and the return process. It was up to us to decide the branding, the target user, and whether to design it as a web app or mobile app.

I decided to design it as a mobile app for general public use, since the flowchart covered a full rental flow that felt more natural as something people would use on their phone.

Process

How I Approached It

01

Research

Before designing, I looked into what a typical rental app usually contains and how similar rental apps handle things like browsing, availability, and verification. This gave me a sense of what to include and how to structure the flow.

02

Wireframing

I started with placeholder boxes and just the necessary labels and text, following the flowchart closely to make sure every step was accounted for.

03

Higher Fidelity UI

Turning it into a higher fidelity design wasn't a requirement, but since I had extra time, I added real images and refined the UI further. I had this checked by our senior designer, who gave feedback on spacing and text sizes. I revised the wireframe after that, fixing missing pages and improving the overall structure.

Key Decisions

Design Choices

Deciding What Info Goes Where

The flowchart only gave the process, not the actual content. I had to figure out what information each screen needed, like what to ask during KYC (ID upload, selfie for face match, proof of address), what details to show in the rental summary (cost, dates, deposit), and how the date and time range selection should work. This meant thinking through the flow from the user's side, not just following the steps on paper.

Dark Teal Color Scheme

I went with a dark teal base to give it a modern, techy feel, paired with bright light teal and yellow accents for section titles and highlights, so it still felt simple but alive.

Minimal, Clean Layout

I kept the layout minimal and clean throughout, focused on making the core actions easy to find.

Brand and Filter on Homepage

The homepage includes a brand row and a device type filter, both based on what the flowchart called for. The brand row can be scrolled, and the device type filter works as intended.

Outcome

Internship project. My first draft was presented to our senior UI/UX designer just two to three days before the internship ended, alongside a separate development project we were finalizing the hand off for, so time for revisions was tight. She gave feedback to fix the layout and increase text size for mobile, since mobile screens need more breathing room and larger tap targets compared to web. The internship ended before I could apply the revisions, so I took the initiative to go back, revise the design based on that feedback, and improve it on my own.
